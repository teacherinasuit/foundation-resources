Question 1
Pick the grade that surprised you the most — not the worst one, the one that doesn't match how hard you actually worked.

Why is there a gap?
Look at your report card. Point to it.
Question 2
If I called the teacher of your lowest grade right now and asked them to describe you as a student in one sentence — what would they say?

Is that who you actually are?
Be honest. Not what you hope they'd say — what they'd actually say.
Question 3
Look at your highest grade. What did you do differently in that class?

And if your answer is "it's easy" — why isn't every class where you understand the material an A?
Easy and high-performing are not the same thing.
Question 4
What's one thing on this report card you don't want to explain to someone you respect?

What are you going to do about it before the next one?
Name the person. Name the plan.

Your grades tell a story.
Make sure it's yours.

The next report card is a blank page. What you do between now and then is the only thing that matters.
