02

Elementary (K-4)

Play the first two segments, then run the logo activity live. Total time: 10-15 minutes.

How to Use the Video ~4 min video + 10 min activity

Play segments 1-2 only (~4 minutes)

The hook (Davon's "take over the world" story) and the NotebookLM/Marvinville story. Pause after the Marvinville section.

Then run the logo activity live:

  1. Put your school logo on the board/screen.
  2. Ask students: "Describe this logo. Tell me everything you see." Collect responses out loud or on paper.
  3. Pick two -- one short, one detailed. Read them both aloud. Ask: "Which one gives more information?"
  4. If you have ChatGPT access (free version works): paste the short description and show what it generates. Then paste the detailed one. Show the difference.
  5. If you don't have ChatGPT access: the video already shows the blob vs. the reimagined logo. Refer back to it: "Remember what happened in the video? The short description made a blob. The detailed one made something amazing."
Talking Point for This Age Group "AI learns patterns, just like you learn patterns. The better you describe something, the better it understands you. That's why details matter -- in your writing AND when you use AI."
Take-Home Challenge

"Ask a parent or guardian to help you try NotebookLM. Pick something you're curious about -- dinosaurs, space, how cars work -- and see what it teaches you."

What to skip: Segments 5-6 (the equity gap and close). The $70K school / job displacement framing is too abstract for this age. The logo activity already teaches the core lesson.
03

Middle School (5-8)

Play the full video. Then run a 5-10 minute discussion and activity.

How to Use the Video 14 min video + 10 min discussion

Play the full video (~14 minutes)

No pauses needed -- the pacing works for this age group.

After the video, run the discussion + activity:

  1. Quick reaction: "What surprised you?" Let 2-3 students respond. Don't over-facilitate.
  2. Logo activity extension: Have students write their OWN description of the school logo (3 minutes, on paper or device). Collect a few. If you have ChatGPT access, feed them in live and show the results.
  3. Discussion prompt: "What's something you wish you understood better? If you could upload it to NotebookLM and have it explain it to you using something you already love -- anime, sports, music -- what would you pick?"
Take-Home Challenge

"Go to notebooklm.google.com tonight. Upload one thing -- a YouTube video about a topic you're studying, a chapter from your textbook, your class notes. Generate an audio overview. Listen to it. Come back tomorrow and tell me what it taught you."

04

High School (9-12)

Play the full video. Let it land. Then extend the conversation for 10-15 minutes.

How to Use the Video 14 min video + 15 min discussion

Play the full video (~14 minutes)

Don't pre-explain or post-summarize -- the video does the work.

After the video, extend the conversation:

  1. The gap discussion: "The video mentioned a student at a $70,000-a-year school who has a patent and uses AI to build apps. What are you using AI for? Why is there a difference?"
    Let students wrestle with this. Don't rescue them with the answer. The discomfort IS the lesson.
  2. Live NotebookLM exploration (if devices available):
    • Have students open NotebookLM on their phones or laptops.
    • Upload something from a current class -- reading assignment, study guide, textbook chapter, a YouTube lecture link.
    • Generate an audio overview or study guide together.
    • Discuss: "Is this cheating? Or is this studying? Where's the line?"
  3. If no devices available: Run the discussion only. The video has already planted the seed. Students who are curious will explore NotebookLM on their own.
Take-Home Challenge

"Upload your most confusing class material into NotebookLM. Tell it to explain it using something you actually care about. See if it changes how you understand it. If it does -- why aren't you doing this every day?"

05

For Teachers

This video is for students. But if you're watching it too, here's what to take away.

The Message Under the Message

The logo activity is a writing lesson in disguise

Students are practicing descriptive writing, revision, and precision -- and they don't even realize it. You can run this activity in any subject, any grade.

NotebookLM is not just for students

Upload your curriculum documents, rubrics, or textbook chapters. Generate study guides, audio overviews, or mind maps for your classes in minutes. It's a planning tool, a differentiation tool, and a student support tool.

The assignment test

Paste any assignment into ChatGPT. If it completes it in under 2 minutes, that assignment is measuring compliance, not thinking. That's not a criticism -- it's information. Use it to redesign.
